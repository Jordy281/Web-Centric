DESCRIPTION

This hands-on course examines the technologies and infrastructure required to support electronic commerce. Key web concepts and skills for creating and maintaining websites such as XHTML, CSS, PHP, and SQL, ad major componentes and concepts of e-commerce infrastructure such as networking principles, and databases, will be explored in detail through a combination of lectures, in-class examples, individual lab work, assignments, and a final group project.

Academic Honesty

All work you submit must be your own. It is fine to discuss problems with other students, but when it comes time to submit solutions, the materials you hand in must be done individually, by yourself. Any materials referenced must be attributed. Simply, you must clearly indicate any parts, of work you submit, that you did not create. All suspected instances of academic dishonesty will be dealt with according to university policy.

For further information regarding academic honesty at Dalhousie, please see the university plagarism website.

Late Submission Policy

Late assignments are not accepted. However, no penalty will be assessed for assignments that are late due to documented situations (e.g., medical emergencies).
